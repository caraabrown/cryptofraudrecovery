Recovery Protocol

Click a step number to mark it complete. Work through these in order — sequence matters.

This is not legal advice. Consult an attorney for your specific situation. Filing reports costs nothing and creates the legal paper trail that makes recovery possible.
1
Do This Today

Stop All Deposits Immediately

Do not send any more money to the platform under any circumstances. Scammers will attempt to collect more by telling you that you need to pay "taxes," "verification fees," or "insurance" to unlock your frozen funds. These are additional theft mechanisms. There is no fee that will unlock your funds.

2
Do This Today

Preserve All Evidence — Screenshot Everything

Do this before the site disappears. Screenshot and save with timestamps visible:

  • Account balance screens — before and after any withdrawal attempt
  • Trading interface — CALL/PUT buttons, countdown timers, fake price charts, any "profit" display
  • Deposit history — every deposit record the platform shows
  • Withdrawal rejection / fee screens — any message blocking your withdrawal
  • Account information page — showing your account number, user ID, and any identifiers
  • ALL recruitment messages — WhatsApp, WeChat, Telegram, Signal, text messages. Screenshot the full conversation. Make sure your phone's timestamp/carrier bar is visible at the top of each screen.
  • Text messages showing wallet addresses — the wallet address sent to you may differ from what the site shows later. Both are evidence.
  • Group chats — if you were added to a group chat of "investors," screenshot the full chat history including member lists
  • The platform URL in your browser bar — visible in every screenshot
Preserve original files — not screenshots of screenshots.

If someone else has evidence on their phone, have them AirDrop or share the original files — not take a photo of their screen. Original files contain EXIF metadata (timestamps, device info) that is court-admissible. A screenshot of a screenshot destroys that data.

HOW TO GET THE PLATFORM'S SOURCE CODE (these sites block right-click Save As on purpose)
  1. Open the platform page in Chrome or Firefox
  2. Press F12 (Windows) or Cmd+Option+I (Mac) to open Developer Tools
  3. Click the Console tab
  4. Type this command exactly: copy(document.documentElement.outerHTML)
  5. Press Enter — the entire page source is now in your clipboard
  6. Paste into a text editor and save as a .txt file
  7. Paste the code into Claude.ai for analysis — Claude can identify fake trading infrastructure, missing withdrawal functions, and other technical evidence of fraud directly in the code

Do this for every page — deposit page, trading page, wallet page. Identical page structures across multiple pages is itself evidence of fraud.

3
File First

File with the FBI — IC3.gov

Go to ic3.gov and file your complaint. This takes 15–20 minutes. Save your IC3 complaint number — you will use it in every other filing. For losses over $100,000, the IC3 Recovery Asset Team can initiate emergency freeze procedures.

4
Within 48 Hours

File with the FTC

Go to reportfraud.ftc.gov — select Investment Scam → Cryptocurrency. Reference your IC3 number. The FTC aggregates reports to identify patterns and can issue consumer alerts.

5
Within 48 Hours

File with Your State Regulator

California residents: file at dfpi.ca.gov/submit-a-complaint (DFPI) and oag.ca.gov (Attorney General). Other states: search "[your state] financial consumer complaint" to find the right agency. Include your IC3 number in all filings.

6
Within 48 Hours

File with the CFTC — Binary Options & Crypto Fraud

The Commodity Futures Trading Commission has direct jurisdiction over unregistered binary options platforms and crypto commodity fraud. File two separate complaints:

  • CFTC Whistleblower: cftc.gov/ConsumerProtection/FileaTip — complete this first as a prerequisite
  • CFTC Consumer Complaint: forms.cftc.gov/Forms/Complaint — file after the whistleblower form

Check these boxes on the complaint form: "Fraudulent representations," "Cheating or fraud after deposit," "Should be registered but is not," "Option on a commodity," "Digital currencies," and "Binary option." In the description, explicitly state the platform used CALL/PUT countdown timer binary options — this is the CFTC's strongest jurisdictional hook. Reference your IC3 number in both filings.

7
Within 48 Hours

File with the SEC — Securities Fraud

If the platform represented itself as an investment opportunity with promised returns, that may constitute an unregistered securities offering. File at:

  • SEC Tips, Complaints & Referrals

Select "Investment Fraud" and describe the platform's representations about investment returns. Include your IC3 and CFTC complaint numbers. The SEC coordinates with the CFTC on cases that overlap both jurisdictions.

8
This Week

Report All Wallet Addresses to Chainabuse.com

Go to chainabuse.com/report and submit every crypto wallet address associated with the fraud. Submit each address separately.

  • Select category: Pig Butchering Scam
  • Include the platform name, domains, and your IC3/CFTC reference numbers in the description
  • Submit the deposit wallets, any sweep/laundering destination addresses, and all USDT/TRC20/ERC20 addresses shown on the platform

Public chainabuse flags trigger exchange-level screening — if the scammer ever tries to cash out through a licensed exchange (Coinbase, Kraken, etc.), the flagged wallet may trigger a compliance hold.

Also submit all platform domains to Google Safe Browsing to protect future victims searching for the platform.

9
Within 48 Hours

Contact Your Bank or Payment App

If you sent funds via Zelle, wire transfer, or bank ACH: call your bank's fraud line immediately and request a fraud dispute. If you used Cash App, Venmo, or PayPal: use their in-app fraud reporting. Provide your IC3 complaint number. Banks are more responsive when a federal complaint exists. Use the pre-written letter in the "Build Your Reports" tab.

10
Within 72 Hours

Contact Your Crypto Exchange

If you used Coinbase, Kraken, Binance.US, or another licensed exchange to send funds: contact their fraud/compliance team with the destination wallet address and your TX hash. Exchanges have blockchain tracing tools and may be able to flag the receiving wallet or cooperate with law enforcement.

11
This Week

Consider a Crypto Fraud Attorney

For losses above $5,000, consult a crypto fraud or asset recovery attorney. Many work on contingency (no fee unless they recover funds). Civil litigation, DOJ forfeiture proceedings, and OFAC-related claims are all viable paths. The DOJ's November 2025 Scam Center Strike Force has seized over $400M — victims can file claims against seized assets.

12
Ongoing

Write to Your Elected Officials

Congressional pressure has driven every major enforcement action against these operations. Use the "Demand Action" tab to generate personalized letters to the Senate Banking Committee, House Financial Services Committee, and your state's federal senators. Volume matters — 500 constituent letters create urgency that individual complaints do not.

Technical Evidence of Fraud

This section documents why DSJ Exchange (tra818.tw) is a fraudulent shell with no real trading infrastructure. Share this with law enforcement, attorneys, and your bank.

Summary Finding

DSJ Exchange (DSJEX), operating at tra818.tw and affiliated with the "BG Wealth Sharing" operation, is a front-end shell website with no real cryptocurrency trading infrastructure. All account balances, trading charts, and price data displayed on the platform are fabricated. The site is designed to accept deposits and block all withdrawals. This is consistent with a "pig butchering" (sha zhu pan) confidence fraud scheme.

Finding 1 — Identical Page Architecture

The Deposit page, Futures Trading page, and Asset Wallet page all have byte-for-byte identical HTML structure. A real exchange would have fundamentally different infrastructure for each function.

All three pages load from the same shell — no unique functionality per section.

Finding 2 — ECharts Used for "Trading" Data

Every page loads 6 copies of the ECharts JavaScript library. ECharts is a data visualization tool — it draws charts. It is not a trading engine, not an order book, and not connected to any real market feed.

Charts are drawn by a graphics library. They do not reflect real trades.

Finding 3 — Web3 Library is Decorative Only

The Web3.js library (used for real blockchain interaction) appears as a "prefetch" link only — meaning it is loaded as a visual decoy but never actually executed during page load. There is no functional blockchain connection.

Web3 is listed but not active. No real blockchain connectivity exists.

Finding 4 — Copy-Paste Construction

A Cloudflare analytics beacon token is identical across all pages of the site. This is a single token that should be unique per site — its repetition confirms the site was built by copy-pasting a single template.

Token 2534f64338f6431c9fdd0da917dcf8c3 repeated on every page.

Finding 5 — No Real Backend Infrastructure

The entire site is a single-page application (SPA) shell. All logic is loaded from external JavaScript bundles on their own server, which can be deleted or rewritten at any time. There is no evidence of a real exchange backend, order matching engine, or custody infrastructure.

Platform can be shut down and all records erased instantly.

Finding 6 — reCAPTCHA Hidden by Design

The site's source code explicitly hides Google's reCAPTCHA badge using CSS (width:0, height:0). This technique hides security verification from users while potentially using it to avoid automated fraud detection systems.

Security UI deliberately concealed from victims.

Finding 7 — Withdrawal Blocked Without Cause

Account freezes on this platform are applied unilaterally, with no legal basis, no regulatory disclosure, and no appeal process. This is the operational signature of a pig butchering scam — deposits are always enabled; withdrawals are structurally impossible.

No real exchange freezes accounts without documented regulatory cause.

Finding 8 — Consistent with Known Scam Architecture

This platform's structure matches documented patterns in FBI, FTC, and INTERPOL reports on pig butchering operations: fake exchange UI, ECharts-rendered fake price data, Web3 branding without function, and a deposit-only money flow.

Matches FBI and FTC-documented pig butchering platform template exactly.

What this means for your report: You can state in your IC3, FTC, and DFPI filings that you have independently obtained and analyzed the platform's HTML source code, which confirms the absence of real trading infrastructure. Attach screenshots of the source code alongside your complaints. The technical evidence supports a finding of intentional fraud, not negligence.

About Pig Butchering Scams

Context to include in your reports and share with others.

"Pig butchering" (sha zhu pan) is a long-duration investment confidence fraud originating in Southeast Asia. Criminal networks — many operating from forced labor compounds in Cambodia, Myanmar, and Laos — recruit victims through social media, dating apps, and messaging platforms. They build trust over weeks or months before introducing a "trading platform" that appears legitimate. Victims are encouraged to deposit progressively larger sums. When they attempt to withdraw, accounts are frozen. The FBI estimates $5.8 billion was lost by American victims in 2024 alone. The UN estimates over 200,000 people are held in scam compounds, many as trafficking victims forced to run these operations.

In November 2025, the DOJ established a dedicated Scam Center Strike Force and has since seized over $400 million in cryptocurrency. In April 2026, a coordinated takedown resulted in 276 arrests. Victims who file IC3 complaints may be eligible to file claims against seized assets.
